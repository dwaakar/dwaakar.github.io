The project “Strengthening the Implementation of Vehicle Emission and Inspection System through Increased Roadside Monitoring and Sensitization” was implemented by Clean up Nepal from March to August 2019 under the Data for Development (D4D) Program, supported by The Asia Foundation and Development Initiatives. The initiative aimed to address the growing challenge of vehicular emissions in the Kathmandu Valley by improving collaboration among key government agencies, enhancing public awareness, and promoting data-driven advocacy.

The project focused on three major components: stakeholder engagement, sensitization and awareness, and policy-level dialogue. Clean up Nepal strategically engaged institutions such as the Department of Transport Management (DoTM), Vehicle Fitness Test Centers (VFTCs), Metropolitan Traffic Police Division (MTPD), Department of Environment (DoEnv), and several civil society organizations to understand gaps in the current emission inspection system and identify collaborative solutions.

**Key Achievements of the Project**

1. **Strengthened Stakeholder Collaboration**

* Successfully engaged major government bodies including DoTM, MTPD, DoEnv, VFTCs, and CSOs, fostering improved cooperation for vehicle emission monitoring.
* Secured verbal buy‑in from all key stakeholders during the round‑table consultation, establishing a foundation for long‑term collaboration.

**2. Comprehensive Sensitization and Awareness Programs**

* Developed and deployed data‑driven advocacy materials such as presentations, infographics, stickers, placards, and smart street signboards.
* Conducted sensitization training for 16 staff members from DoTM, MTPD, VFTCs, and FoNNTE with a focus on causes, impacts, and solutions to vehicular pollution.

**3. Large-Scale Roadside Awareness Campaign**

* Mobilized and trained 20 volunteers for roadside sensitization activities across strategic locations in Kathmandu Valley.
* Reached and sensitized approximately 2,000 vehicles (public and private) through direct interaction.
* Distributed 5,000 stickers during roadside campaigns and 1,500 stickers during stakeholder trainings for wider public messaging

**4. Data Collection and Evidence-Based Insights**

* Conducted a roadside survey of 1,900 vehicles, generating critical insights:
* 20% of vehicles were identified as gross emitters.
* 9% were in very poor mechanical condition.
* 11.8% of vehicle owners lacked basic maintenance knowledge.
* Used the collected data during the round‑table discussion to highlight system gaps and influence decision-making.

**5. High-Level Policy Dialogue and Recommendations**

* Organized a round‑table discussion with 16 officials representing key agencies to deliberate on emission control, green sticker integrity, and long‑term policy measures.
* Developed actionable recommendations, including improving testing infrastructure, enhancing interagency communication, increasing fixed emission testing stations, and promoting long‑term public awareness strategies.
