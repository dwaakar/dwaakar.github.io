This study investigates the impacts of cascade dams on sediment transport and distribution within the Marsyangdi River Basin (MRB), a critical yet complex watershed in the Himalayas. With the ongoing development of hydropower projects in the MRB, understanding sediment dynamics is essential to balance energy production. The study aims to assess sediment load and analyze how cascade damming influences sediment grain size distribution and transport patterns. Data were collected through field sampling, surveys, and remote sensing, focusing on sediment characteristics and hydrological responses across multiple dam sites within the basin. Field sampling of the sediment incurred a yield of 0.052 Mt/a (megatons per annum) for the year 2023/24. Hydrological modeling was carried out using the Soil and Water Assessment Tool (SWAT), The findings of the model reveal a reduction of a 24.7% decrease in total sediment yield from the previous study. The longitudinal variation in the sediment characteristics depicts significant disruption due to cascade damming in the natural sediment flow in the pre-monsoon season. This led to altered sediment grain size distribution, with finer sediments (~954% finer than that of the natural river) being trapped in the dam and backwater region and coarser sediment being released in the lower reaches of the river stretch. These changes impact river morphology, water quality, and aquatic habitats, with potential consequences for hydropower efficiency. The KII in the hydropower plant revealed enough execution of sediment management in the HPP and dam region with high and low flow management, presence of desanders, and monitoring of sediment in the monsoon. The cascade dams have substantially modified sediment dynamics in the MRB, particularly generating a lag in sediment transport necessitating integrated sediment management strategies. The implications are profound for hydropower project planning and environmental management, highlighting the need for policies that mitigate the adverse effects of sediment retention while optimizing energy generation. To explore the long-term impacts of these changes on river ecosystems and to refine sediment management practices in the context of ongoing and future hydropower developments, further research is recommended.
