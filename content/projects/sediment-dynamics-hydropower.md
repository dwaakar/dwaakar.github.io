This thesis assessed sediment load and transport mechanisms in the Marsyangdi River Basin and evaluated the environmental impacts of peaking run-of-river hydropower plants. The research combined field sampling, GIS analysis, and hydrological modelling to quantify sediment fluxes and identify how hydropower operations alter natural sediment regimes — with implications for downstream ecology, infrastructure longevity, and riparian communities. A peer-reviewed journal article from this work has been submitted to the Journal of Sedimentary Environments.
