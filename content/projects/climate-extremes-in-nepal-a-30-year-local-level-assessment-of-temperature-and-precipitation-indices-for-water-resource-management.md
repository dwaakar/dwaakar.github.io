Nepal's extreme altitudinal gradient, spanning subtropical Terai plains to trans-Himalayan peaks exceeding 8,000 m, makes it one of the world's most climatically heterogeneous and vulnerable nations. Yet localised climate evidence aligned with Nepal's 753 federally empowered local government units, known as palikas (local level), has remained unavailable, creating a critical scale mismatch for adaptation planning. This study presents the first comprehensive assessment of climate extremes across all 753 local levels using 12 ETCCDI indices computed from ERA5-Land reanalysis data over the period 1995-2024. Seven precipitation indices (PRCPTOT, SDII, CDD, R20mm, RX7day, R95pTOT, R99pTOT) and five temperature indices (TMm, TXm, TXgt50p, TX3TN3, TXb3TNb3) were calculated at the grid-point scale using an index-then-aggregate approach, with long-term trends assessed through linear regression and spatial climate regimes identified through K-means and hierarchical clustering. Results confirm unidirectional national warming: 56.44% of local levels show statistically significant increases in mean daily temperature, with warming rates reaching +0.055 degree C/year in high-altitude western local levels, providing direct observational evidence of Elevation-Dependent Warming. A 51% increase in consecutive hot day-night periods and a 55% reduction in cold-spell frequency reinforced this thermal transformation. Precipitation signals are spatially divergent: the central and eastern mid-hills are experiencing intensification of heavy rainfall, daily intensity, and extreme-event contribution, while the eastern and far-western Terai show significant declines in total precipitation and intensity alongside exclusively lengthening dry spells. Clustering confirms three spatially coherent climate regimes corresponding to the Himalayan, mid-hill, and Terai physiographic zones. The local level-level trend data and cluster assignments generated by this study provide an actionable evidence base for Local Adaptation Plans of Action and trans-boundary watershed management across Nepal's diverse climatic landscape.
